# Review: SUGGESTIONS

## Required Changes
1. **Prompt-change baseline**: Resume is still comparing against mutable project metadata instead of the interrupted cycle’s stored prompt baseline.
   - Current: [engine.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/workflow_composition/engine.rs#L903) passes `project_record.prompt_hash` into the prompt-drift check, while the intended per-run field lives on [model.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/project_run_record/model.rs#L101) and paused runs clear `active_run` at [engine.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/workflow_composition/engine.rs#L3814). The Slice 5 field is not what resume actually uses.
   - Expected: Persist or reconstruct the interrupted run’s `prompt_hash_at_cycle_start` and compare `prompt.md` against that value during resume.
   - Reference: Slice 5 acceptance criterion for `evaluate_prompt_change_on_resume()` comparing against `prompt_hash_at_cycle_start`; Recovery and Failure Precedence prompt-change rule.

2. **Final-review optional-member handling**: An optional reviewer that fails during proposal collection can still re-enter the stage during voting.
   - Current: [final_review.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/workflow_composition/final_review.rs#L247) skips optional proposal failures, but voting later iterates the full `panel.reviewers` list again at [final_review.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/workflow_composition/final_review.rs#L400). That lets a reviewer who was supposed to be skipped still affect vote totals and consensus.
   - Expected: Carry forward only the reviewers that successfully completed the proposal phase, or otherwise make the proposal-phase skip sticky for the rest of final review.
   - Reference: Slice 5 implementation notes: optional reviewer failures should be skipped if `min_reviewers` remains satisfied.

3. **Final-review invocation identity**: Final-review panel members do not get unique `invocation_id`s.
   - Current: [final_review.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/contexts/workflow_composition/final_review.rs#L773) builds `invocation_id` from only stage, panel role, and cursor, so reviewer proposals collide with each other and planner/reviewer votes collide with each other. Those IDs are used as raw-output filenames at [fs.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/adapters/fs.rs#L887) and as process-backend child/temp-file keys at [process_backend.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/adapters/process_backend.rs#L218), [process_backend.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/adapters/process_backend.rs#L451), and [process_backend.rs](/root/new-ralph-burning/ralph-burning-rewrite/src/adapters/process_backend.rs#L627).
   - Expected: Include a stable per-member discriminator such as reviewer index, planner, or arbiter in the final-review invocation IDs so per-member raw output, temp files, and cancellation bookkeeping stay distinct.
   - Reference: Slice 5 final-review panel orchestration and the prompt’s review/debug fidelity requirement.
